7 Day Kruger National Park All Inclusive Safari

This 7 day Kruger National Park all inclusive safari offers the most complete and immersive experience in the park. With six nights spent inside Kruger, you’ll explore multiple regions at a relaxed pace while maximising your time in the bush.

From the open plains of central Kruger to the river systems of the south and the rugged landscapes near Berg-en-Dal, this safari gives you the time and flexibility to fully experience the park. It’s the ideal choice for travellers who want to slow down, explore in depth, and enjoy a true wilderness experience. 7 Day Safari Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival and transfer into Kruger National Park, followed by an afternoon game drive en route to camp.

Day 2: Full day exploring the central Kruger region, known for open plains, predators, and excellent general game viewing.

Day 3: Continue your safari through the park with more time to explore key routes and fully enjoy sightings at a relaxed pace.

Day 4: Travel south through Kruger with game viewing along the way, experiencing a clear change in habitat and wildlife.

Day 5: Full day exploring southern Kruger, an area known for river systems, diverse wildlife, and strong Big Five potential.

Day 6: Spend your final full day on safari with flexible game drives shaped around your interests and current wildlife activity.

Day 7: Optional early morning drive and transfer to the airport.

The Full Kruger Safari Experience

This isn’t a rushed, checklist-style safari. With seven days in Kruger National Park, you have the time to slow down, settle into the rhythm of the bush, and experience the park in a far deeper and more rewarding way.

Instead of moving quickly from sighting to sighting, we take the time to observe behaviour, follow animal movement, and let each encounter unfold naturally. Whether it’s spending longer with a lion pride, following elephants through a riverbed, or sitting quietly at a productive waterhole, the extra time allows the safari to feel richer and more complete.

Staying inside the park makes this experience even more immersive. Early mornings, late afternoons, and the sounds of the bush at night all become part of your safari — not just the game drives themselves. Over a full week, you begin to experience the different moods, habitats, and rhythms of Kruger in a way that shorter safaris simply cannot offer. The experience varies throughout the year, and you can read more about the best time to visit Kruger National Park to plan your trip.

With a private guide and a flexible itinerary, each day adapts to you. Some days may be filled with unforgettable sightings, others may be slower and more reflective — but over seven days, that balance creates the most complete Kruger safari experience we offer.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 7 days enough in Kruger National Park? ⌄

Yes, a 7-day safari allows you to explore multiple regions of Kruger National Park at a relaxed pace. This increases your chances of seeing the Big Five while enjoying diverse habitats and excellent photographic opportunities.

Is this 7-day safari private? ⌄

Yes, this is a fully private safari. You will have your own professional guide, private vehicle, and flexible schedule tailored to your interests.

Which rest camps do you stay at during the 7-day safari? ⌄

This 7-day safari typically includes three different rest camps in Kruger National Park, such as Satara, Skukuza or Lower Sabie, and Berg-en-Dal. This allows you to experience different wildlife regions.

What is included in the 7-day all inclusive safari? ⌄

The safari includes accommodation inside Kruger National Park, all meals, daily game drives, conservation fees, private guide, and airport transfers. We handle all logistics so you can focus on the wildlife experience.

What is the best time of year for a 7-day Kruger safari? ⌄

A 7-day safari works well year-round. The dry winter months offer excellent Big Five viewing, while summer provides lush landscapes, birdlife, and newborn animals.

Can the 7-day itinerary be customised? ⌄

Yes, all safaris are flexible. We can adjust rest camps, focus on photography, birding, or specific wildlife interests to suit your travel preferences.
