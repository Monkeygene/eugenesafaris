Best Time to Visit Kruger National Park for Safari

Our Kruger National Park all inclusive safari packages run year-round, and each season offers a completely different experience. The best time to visit depends on what you want to see — whether it's peak wildlife viewing, birdlife, photography, or lush landscapes. You can explore detailed monthly conditions in our Kruger National Park weather guide .

Dry Season (May to September)

The dry season is widely regarded as the best time for wildlife viewing in Kruger National Park. As vegetation thins and water sources become limited, animals gather around rivers and waterholes, making sightings more consistent and easier to follow.

  • Excellent Big Five sightings and predator activity
  • Cooler temperatures and comfortable game drives
  • Less vegetation for clearer visibility
  • Lower mosquito activity

Green Season (October to April)

The green season transforms Kruger into a vibrant, lush landscape. This is the best time for birding, photography, and seeing newborn animals. While wildlife can be more challenging to spot due to thicker vegetation, the overall experience is rich, colourful, and full of life.

  • Lush scenery and dramatic skies
  • Excellent birding with migratory species
  • Newborn animals and active ecosystems
  • Ideal for photographers and filmmakers

If you're unsure which season suits your safari best, you can read our full guide on the best time to visit Kruger National Park , or get in touch and we’ll help you plan your safari based on your interests, travel dates, and expectations.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is included in a Kruger National Park all inclusive safari packages? ⌄

Our all inclusive safaris include accommodation inside Kruger National Park, all meals, game drives, conservation fees, and a professional guide. We also handle transfers and logistics.

How many days should I spend in Kruger National Park? ⌄

We recommend at least 5 days for the best wildlife experience. Longer safaris increase your chances of seeing the Big Five and allow you to explore different regions of the park.

Do you stay inside Kruger National Park? ⌄

Yes, all our safaris stay inside Kruger National Park rest camps, allowing early morning and late afternoon game drives for optimal wildlife viewing.

Are your safaris private? ⌄

Yes, our safaris are fully private. You will have your own guide, vehicle, and flexible schedule tailored to your interests.

When is the best time to visit Kruger National Park? ⌄

The dry winter months from May to September offer excellent wildlife viewing, but Kruger is a year-round destination with great birding and lush landscapes in summer.
