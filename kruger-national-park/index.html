Kruger National Park

Kruger National Park is one of Africa’s greatest safari destinations, covering nearly 2 million hectares of protected wilderness in South Africa. Known for its exceptional wildlife, diverse landscapes, and reliable Big Five sightings, the park offers a safari experience that is both accessible and deeply rewarding for first-time visitors and seasoned travellers alike.

Whether you choose a private safari or a fully all-inclusive Kruger safari, the experience is shaped by the rhythm of the bush — from early morning game drives to quiet moments at waterholes as the day unfolds.

In this guide, you’ll discover what makes Kruger National Park so special, from its size and ecosystems to the wildlife, seasons, and safari options that define the experience.

How Big is Kruger National Park?

Kruger National Park covers an incredible 19,485 square kilometers (7,523 square miles), making it one of the largest and most diverse wildlife reserves in Africa. To put it into perspective, it is roughly the size of Israel or the US state of New Jersey.

The park stretches from the Crocodile River in the south to the Limpopo River in the north, forming part of the Greater Limpopo Transfrontier Park, which connects South Africa with Mozambique and Zimbabwe.

This vast scale creates an extraordinary range of habitats, from open savanna and mopane woodland to riverine forests and rocky outcrops. It is this diversity that makes Kruger National Park one of the most rewarding safari destinations in Africa. Explore this landscape on a guided Kruger safari .

Wildlife: What Can You Expect to See?

Kruger National Park is one of the best places in Africa to see wildlife. The park is home to the famous Big Five — lion, leopard, elephant, buffalo, and both black and white rhinoceros.

Beyond the Big Five, Kruger supports an incredible variety of life. You can expect regular sightings of giraffe, zebra, wildebeest, and antelope, as well as predators like cheetah, hyena, and wild dog in certain areas. The park is also a birding hotspot, with over 500 species recorded throughout the year.

The diversity of habitats across Kruger means every drive is different, with new sightings around every corner. With an experienced guide, you not only increase your chances of seeing more wildlife, but also gain a deeper understanding of animal behaviour and the smaller details that bring the bush to life.

Where to Stay: Camps & Safari Packages

Kruger National Park offers a range of accommodation options, but the experience varies greatly depending on how you choose to explore the park.

  • SANParks Rest Camps such as Skukuza, Lower Sabie, Satara, and Letaba provide a classic Kruger experience and form the base for many guided safaris.
  • Private Lodges in surrounding reserves offer a more exclusive and luxury-focused safari environment.
  • Fully guided safari experiences — including our all-inclusive Kruger safaris and private guided safaris — combine carefully selected accommodation with expert guiding, allowing you to focus entirely on the experience.

Choosing a guided safari brings everything together, from where you stay to how you experience the park. Explore our full range of Kruger National Park safaris to find the right fit for your trip.

Best Time to Visit Kruger National Park

Kruger National Park is a year-round safari destination , but the experience changes with the seasons. The dry winter months from May to September are often the best time for game viewing, as vegetation is thinner and animals gather more regularly around rivers, dams, and waterholes.

The green season, from October to April, brings a very different side of the park. This is a beautiful time for birding, dramatic skies, newborn animals, and lush landscapes that completely transform the feel of Kruger.

Getting There: Kruger National Park

Reaching Kruger National Park is straightforward, with several well-connected airports providing easy access to the region. Most safaris begin with a short flight followed by a transfer into the park.

The most commonly used airports include Skukuza Airport, Kruger Mpumalanga International Airport (KMIA), and Eastgate Airport. Each offers convenient connections from Johannesburg and Cape Town.

On a guided safari, transfers and logistics are taken care of for you, making the journey into Kruger smooth and stress-free. Explore our all-inclusive Kruger safaris to see how your trip can be seamlessly arranged from arrival to departure.

Malaria & Health

Kruger National Park is located in a low-risk malaria area, particularly during the warmer summer months. While the risk is generally low, it is always a good idea to speak with a medical professional before travelling and take simple precautions such as using insect repellent and wearing long sleeves in the evenings.

On a guided safari, your experience is designed to be both comfortable and safe, with expert guidance throughout your time in the park. This allows you to focus on enjoying the wildlife and the environment with confidence.

Many travellers visit Kruger every year without issue, making it one of the most accessible safari destinations in Africa.
