Kruger National Park Rest Camps

Image

Berg-en-Dal Rest Camp

Nestled in the southwestern corner of Kruger, Berg-en-Dal boasts a picturesque setting amidst rocky hills and dry riverbeds. Established in 1984, it's one of the park's newer camps and offers modern facilities, including a restaurant, shop, and swimming pool. The camp's location provides excellent opportunities to spot white rhinos, leopards, and a variety of bird species.

Crocodile Bridge Rest Camp

Situated near the southeastern border, Crocodile Bridge serves as both a rest camp and an entrance gate. Its proximity to the Crocodile River makes it a hotspot for birdwatching and game viewing. The camp offers basic amenities and is ideal for travelers seeking a quieter, more intimate experience.

Image

Letaba Rest Camp

Located on the banks of the Letaba River, this camp is renowned for its lush vegetation and abundant wildlife. Letaba is home to the Elephant Hall Museum, showcasing the park's elephant history. The camp offers a range of accommodations, from campsites to guest houses, and facilities like a restaurant, shop, and swimming pool.

View Rest Camp
Image

Lower Sabie Rest Camp

Positioned along the Sabie River, Lower Sabie offers stunning river views and is known for its rich biodiversity. The camp provides various accommodations, including safari tents and bungalows, and amenities like a restaurant, shop, and swimming pool. It's a favorite among visitors for its excellent game viewing opportunities.

Image

Mopani Rest Camp

Established in 1992, Mopani is one of the newer camps, situated near the Pioneer Dam in the northern region. The camp is characterized by its mopane tree surroundings and offers facilities such as a restaurant, shop, and swimming pool. It's an excellent base for exploring the northern parts of the park.

Image

Olifants Rest Camp

Perched atop a hill, Olifants provides panoramic views of the Olifants River below. The camp's elevated position offers unique wildlife viewing opportunities, including sightings of elephants and hippos. Facilities include a restaurant, shop, and various accommodation options.

View Rest Camp
Image

Orpen Rest Camp

Located at the park's western entrance, Orpen is a smaller camp that offers a cozy atmosphere. It's known for its well-maintained gardens and frequent wildlife sightings right at the camp's perimeter. Amenities include a shop, fuel station, and swimming pool.

View Rest Camp
Image

Pretoriuskop Rest Camp

As the oldest rest camp in Kruger, Pretoriuskop is steeped in history and charm. Situated in the southwestern region, it's surrounded by granite outcrops and unique vegetation. The camp offers a range of accommodations, a restaurant, shop, and a natural rock swimming pool.

View Rest Camp
Image

Punda Maria Rest Camp

Located in the far north, Punda Maria is known for its rich birdlife and proximity to the Pafuri region. The camp offers various accommodations, a shop, and a swimming pool. It's an excellent base for exploring the park's northernmost areas and experiencing its unique flora and fauna.

View Rest Camp
Image

Satara Rest Camp

Situated in the central region, Satara is renowned for its open plains and high concentrations of predators, especially lions. The camp offers a variety of accommodations, a restaurant, shop, and swimming pool. It's a favorite among wildlife enthusiasts seeking thrilling game viewing experiences.

View Rest Camp
Image

Shingwedzi Rest Camp

Located in the northern mopane woodlands, Shingwedzi offers a tranquil setting along the Shingwedzi River. The camp provides various accommodations, a restaurant, shop, and swimming pool. It's known for its elephant sightings and serene atmosphere.

View Rest Camp
Image

Skukuza Rest Camp

As the largest and most developed camp, Skukuza serves as the administrative headquarters of Kruger National Park. Situated on the banks of the Sabie River, it offers a wide range of accommodations, multiple restaurants, shops, a golf course, and even a conference center. Skukuza is a bustling hub and a great starting point for exploring the park.
