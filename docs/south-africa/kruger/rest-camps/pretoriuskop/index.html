Pretoriuskop Rest Camp

Pretoriuskop Rest Camp, located in the southwestern corner of Kruger National Park near Numbi Gate, is the park's oldest and most historic camp. Surrounded by granite outcrops and rich grasslands, it offers a range of accommodation options—from campsites and traditional huts to cozy bungalows and spacious guest houses—suiting all types of travelers. The camp features excellent facilities, including a restaurant, shop, swimming pool, laundromat, and picnic areas, all designed to provide comfort while maintaining a rustic, authentic safari feel. Guests can enjoy guided game drives, bush walks, birdwatching, and photography, making it a perfect base for wildlife enthusiasts. With its deep historical roots, restored heritage structures, and unique gardens planted by early rangers, Pretoriuskop blends nostalgia with natural beauty for a relaxed and memorable Kruger experience.

Location

Proximity to Gates

  • 9 km from Numbi Gate
  • 24 km from Phabeni Gate
  • 49 km from Skukuza Rest Camp

Landscape

Nestled atop rocky granite outcrops and hills, Pretoriuskop offers stunning panoramic views born of ancient molten rock formations.

Vegetation

Surrounded by thick woodlands, tall sourveld grasses and patches of sweeter drainage-line grass, the camp supports a rich biodiversity of flora and fauna.

Strategic Location

Its position in the southwestern corner of Kruger National Park makes Pretoriuskop an ideal base for exploring the park’s southern regions.

History

Oldest Rest Camp

Pretoriuskop was the first camp opened to visitors in 1927, marking the beginning of tourism in Kruger National Park.

Name Origin

Named after Voortrekker Willem Pretorius—part of Carl Trichardt's 1848 expedition—whose grave lies near the camp.

Historical Structures

The Wolhuter Hut, built in 1930 as one of the original tourist huts, is currently being restored to its original form.

Unique Flora

Early ranger Harry Wolhuter planted flamboyant and bougainvillea trees—still flourishing today—to give the camp its distinctive botanical charm.
