Why choose our Kruger National Park safari packages

Our Kruger National Park safari packages focus on the Kruger safari experience, with expert guiding and exceptional wildlife sightings. We do not only want you to see the big 5, but we want you to explore and experience the atmosphere of the park, wide open landscapes and diversity of wildlife.

We do longer days in the park to give you the best chance of seeing the big 5, the big 5 is of course a highlight and the reason most visitors come to Kruger National Park, read more below.

What to expect on our Kruger National Park safari packages

Expect timeless safaris focused on your preference, do you enjoy birdwatching, we can do a birding safari, do you want to focus on photography, we can do a photographic safari, do you want to see the big 5 and explore the park, no worries. Our safari packages in Kruger National Park are all private and flexible, so we can adjust the itinerary on the go or before hand.

The wildlife is why everyone comes to visit the Kruger, so expect to see a lof of wildlife and impala. Expect a slow pace, we want sightings to find us not the other way around. We want to give you the best chance to see the big 5 and any other wildlife you would want to see.

What is included in our Kruger National Park safari packages

All safari packages include expert guiding, private safari vehicle, and accommodation. Our all insclusive safari packages include meals, park fees, and accommodation, while our private safari packages offer more flexibility in terms of meals. You may add any additional elements like photographic safaris, birding safaris, or any other element that sparks your interest.

We do photography for beginners as well, giving you an induction to african wildlife photography, and the same goes for our birding safaris. So what I am saying is the photographic safari option includes photography for beginners too, teaching you how to use your camera and how to get great shots.

Why we recommend longer Kruger National Park safari tour packages

When going on a longer safari in Kruger National Park, you increase your chances of seeing more wildlife and you do not have to feel rushed. This gives us the chance to explore different areas of the park, so that you can focus not only on the wildlife but also taking in the breathtaking open landscapes of the great South African lowveld.

Kruger National Park is a huge national park, spanning over 2 million hectares. I want you to not just explore one part of the park, but experience the diversity of wildlife and atmosphere. a Slow safari allows you to take in everything from the geography to the largest of elephants.
